For the purpose of promoting greater gender equity in music, Lea Bertucci is organizing a database of Women/GNC folks working in music in NY. Spread the word!

23 Essential Underground Dance Music Venues in the United States

Berghain to open Säule floor in March
Thursday
BOUND w/ Juliana Huxtable, Bearcat, Porcelain, Latex Lucifer @ Tilt BK // 10 PM - 4 AM // Fetish attire & queerness strictly enforced at the door. Techno sets from Juliana Huxtable and BEARCAT, joined by Remy Marc and Katie Rex. Free before 11 PM / $10 after for community members / more $$ for squares. Donation jars at event are for Southern Poverty Law Center.
HEFT @ Sunnyvale // midnight - 4 AM // If you're more into plastic than leather, or if you're curious to check out a new party, come to the first edition of HEFT! Featuring deep dark garbage bag techno from Olga, Nick Bazzano, Kiddo Sincere, and Rob Seurat. Free
Friday
Mutual Dreaming presents Dr. Rubinstein (4 hour set) @ Brooklyn Bazaar // 11 PM - 5 AM // Wow! Dr. Rubinstein blew our minds at Sustain Release and now she's back to serenade us for 4 hours with slamming acid techno and dark electro, joined by Akanbi. We will accept only 4 hours, but we would prefer more (she played 9 hours at Berghain last weekend). Adv tix / $? door
Saturday
INDEX Festival: Living Archive @ Knockdown Center // Sat. 4 PM - Sun. 2 AM // INDEX is a 2-day festival of music, performance, and community engagement, highlighting the voices of Black-Latinx artists, activists, and academics as we observe Black History Month. You're invited to engage a catalog of readings, performance, installation, and discussion. Tix
OVA THE RAINBOW - HERMITAGE @ The Dreamhouse - RSVP // 10 PM - ??? AM // Unleash your hermitage lqqk, and then crawl out of your shell to sets from Kiddy Smile, Crypolect, #pozsipozsi, and aCeb00mbaP. Live performances and lovely hosts, with all donations supporting this queer community space. $10 before midnight / $15 after / $20 after 3 AM / $10 all night with INDEX wristband
Bushwick A/V Sunday Showcase: Techno Queers @ 299 Vandervoort Ave. // 4 AM - 2 PM // Want to keep dancing with queer family after OVA THE RAINBOW? We're hosting afters at Bushwick A/V instead of at a random warehouse, so the party will end only after everyone collapses and not because of police! Ne/Re/A, Maŕa, Modest Glesman, Olga, and A Village Raid will twist your mind with darker shades of techno, so unleash your inner freak til sometime after families walk home from church. ***Say "techno queers" at the door for discounted entry all night: $20 (cheaper than adv tix!)***
Sunday
Between 0 and 1: Part Two @ MoMA PS1 // 3PM // Terre Thaemlitz (DJ Sprinkes) performs Cantos I-IV, an extract from Soulnessless, which is actually the longest mass produced album in history (clocking in at over 32 hours). The performance will highlight Thaemlitz's self-critical and fluid identity politic, exploring gender, sexuality, class, linguistics, ethnicity, race, and mobility. At 5 PM, Thaemlitz will join Honey Dijon in a conversation re: non-essentialist approaches to life and art, moderated by Juliana Huxtable.
Horse Meat Disco - Presidents Day Weekend @ Output // 10 PM - 5 AM // When Output is flooded with sweaty, shirtless gay dudes on a Sunday for HMD, the bro factor vanishes. Celebrate (Not My) Presidents' Day, aka Go Hard If You Don't Have Work Tomorrow Day, with sets from Horse Meat Disco & Sean B in main room, and Ron Like Hell & Bil Todd in the Panther Room. Adv tix / Door $40+
