+ From at least 1978-1981, Brooklyn Heights was home to a gay bar/discotheque called Sal’s, located at 79 Pineapple Street. An ad for the bar notes that it was “The Showplace Of Brooklyn” & was the “Home of the Harlequin Players,” who were performing a show there called “Basically Bette Davis.” [Queer Happened Here]
+ From approximately 1977-1982, Dapper & Friends operated as a lesbian bar & disco at 350 E. 81st Street between First & Second Avenues on Manhattan’s Upper East Side. Notably, the venue was owned & run by a queer woman who went by the nickname “Dapper” & dressed in 3-piece-suits, alongside her former (& significantly older) lover named Sandy. [Queer Happened Here]
+ Palladium was a 104,000-square-foot former cinema that Studio 54 founders Steve Rubell and Ian Schrager transformed into a “multimedia club.” Designed by Japanese architect Arata Isozaki, the interior created a dialogue between the historic shell of the old building and a radically new structure within it. Palladium closed in 1997 and was later demolished to make way for an NYU dorm. [documoda]
+ BREAKING NEWS - R.I.P. Macri Park [Located at 462 Union Ave in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, Macri Park began as a dive bar popular with hipsters & was run by John McGillion, who also owned nearby dives Alligator Lounge, Lulu's, & the Charleston. In 2015, however, he handed Macri Park over to Steven McEnrue of Metropolitan, who rebranded Macri as a gay bar complete with new spotlights, a stage, "club lighting", new furniture & DJ equipment. For the next 11 years, it would serve as a laidback but beloved neighborhood gay bar featuring understated decor & a bare-bones outdoor patio.] [Queer Happened Here]
