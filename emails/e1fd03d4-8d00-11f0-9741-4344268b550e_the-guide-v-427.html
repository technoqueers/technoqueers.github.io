+ My Ballroom Story [Felix Rodriguez and Michael Bullock on ballroom legends of the past and present.] [PW] "Ballroom started uptown, in Harlem, in the ’60s, when it was illegal to be in drag. They would do them incognito in bars that closed at three o’clock in the morning. The ball would start at five AM, because that was the only time that they could perform these events. Gays were considered the lowest of the low. Imagine being in Harlem at 11 o’clock in the morning after a ball had ended, and seeing so many queens parading out of the club wearing all these outfits and boas. It was a different time."
+ Am I on the List? We asked some of New York’s most stylish door people what will get you turned away from the bar. Dress (and act) accordingly. [The Cut] 
What’s the difference between how millennials go out versus Gen Z? 
These kids have no club etiquette. We don’t care how many followers you have, we really don’t. 

People on the internet say nightlife is dead — what’s your response to that? 
If you know where to go and you know where to look, nightlife is not dead. The people saying that don’t know how to have fun. Everyone needs to go outside. Get off your phone. Don’t be on TikTok so much. More people should be going out by themselves. When you stop meeting new people in your life, you’re doing yourself a huge disservice. Go outside alone, grab a drink, and talk to a stranger. Smoke a cigarette with a stranger. Talk to someone older than you — intergenerational connections are everything. This is f—ing New York City. Talk to the people who have been here for a million years and have been keeping this city alive.
