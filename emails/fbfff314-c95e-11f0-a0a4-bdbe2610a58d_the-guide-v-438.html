+ Opened in 1989 by Christina Visca, Richard Grant, & Phil Smith, Sound Factory became one of New York’s most influential late-20th-century dance clubs. Its converted-warehouse home at 530 W. 27th Street was known for a powerful sound system & for its groundbreaking resident DJ, Junior Vasquez, whose ties to the Houses of Xtravaganza & Aviance helped bring Harlem ballroom culture downtown. [Queer Happened Here]
+ From around the mid-1990s into the early 2000s, Girl Zone at 504 West 16th Street served as a key venue for NYC’s roving lesbian & queer women’s nightlife. Located just off Tenth Ave in West Chelsea–still an industrial area then known for large warehouse clubs–the venue offered a consistent Saturday-night space for the lesbian, bisexual & queer community at a time when women-centered parties were frequently itinerant or temporary. [QHH]
+ Refuge is a “hi-def dance club” housed inside a nearly 200-year-old industrial complex at 360 Ten Eyck Street. Co-founded by nightlife veterans John Dimatteo & celebrated sound engineer Craig “Shorty” Bernabeu, the club was built entirely around its custom 10-foot-tall, four-point analog REX sound system–handmade, tuned daily, & designed to recreate the warmth & precision of the great New York club rooms of the past. [QHH]
