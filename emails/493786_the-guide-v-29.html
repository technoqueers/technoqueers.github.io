(( mix of the week ))
(( Honey Dijon ))

Miss Honey Dijon is a force to be reckoned with. Born and raised in Chicago, she cut her teeth playing alongside house legends and close friends Derrick Carter, Ron Hardy, and Mark Farina before relocating to New York in the mid-90s. Once in the city, she translated her deep musical knowledge and DJ chops into several residencies in lower Manhattan’s once-vibrant house scene, and eventually helmed iconic parties such as Body and Soul and Danny Krivit’s 718 Sessions. In the years since, Honey has garnered international attention and played some of the world’s best venues, left her mark on the fashion world through numerous high-profile collaborations, and most recently re-established her residency in Manhattan at Sunday’s debaucherous Battle Hymn party.

As a DJ, Honey favors old-school house, techno, and acid records with throbbing basslines, driving rhythms, and party rockin’ grooves. Her taste is eclectic and mixing ability razor sharp, resulting in sets that brim with intensity but never feel formulaic or cliché.

This week’s mix, recorded for Dutch magazine I-D ahead of her set at Melt festival, is one earth-shaking hour of music for the rave. Chicago classics like Mr. Finger’s “Can You Feel It” blend wonderfully with 909-flavored bangers, tribal house tracks that thud and clang, and soulful bits of melody. The result is irresistibly infectious.

This Friday, catch Honey play alongside fellow Chicagoan DJ Heather and local duo The Long Count Cycle at the final edition of Alone Together, one of our most beloved parties that we are sad to see come to a close.
