+ Liz Pelly Wrote a Book About Spotify and the "Systemic Grift" of Streaming [First Floor] "Pelly debunks Spotify’s repeated claims that the company was set up to “save” the music industry from piracy, details its heavy reliance on user surveillance and data collection, and points out the myriad ways it has actively blurred the lines between artistry and commerce to reduce its own costs. The book is a story of wealth extraction, corporate consolidation and the merciless flattening of music culture. The information revealed is sometimes so frustrating and/or bleak that even already-cynical music fans will likely throw up their hands in aggravation."

"When you talk about criticisms of Spotify, it’s really important to remember that any conversation about the company is inherently also a conversation about the major labels. Spotify and the whole streaming model wouldn’t be possible without the cooperation of the major labels and without the way they’ve pushed to determine the basics of how these platforms are structured."
