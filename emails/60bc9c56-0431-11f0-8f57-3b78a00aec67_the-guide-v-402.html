+ "Everyone should be in a collective" [Ryan C. Clarke] [The Politics of Dancing] "It feels like people aren't really trying to have political conversations anymore, people just want to have a good time again. So that has definitely felt like a regression, at least in my field of Blackness. We love the aesthetics of it, but we don't want to carry on the tradition of disruption or being uncomfortable. [...] For the most part everyone is on their phones, filming videos and Shazaming in the club. We need to cultivate relationships between each other, because how can we address the problems if we don't know how to address each other?"
+ Reflecting on 2 decades of social media promotion [Russell E.L. Butler] "These platforms privilege content that generates the most data. As a result, what I want to create becomes what I should create. To access the highest privileges of social media algorithms, songs should be shorter to fit into short videos or fit into playlists with ease, songs that work should be easy to replicate because they are quickly disposed of, and the only history that the songs should recall is the history created on these apps. [...] I must sacrifice myself and all that I believe, so that these songs gain the favor of the algorithm."

"I have experienced the direct social implications that this process has created. I have witnessed attention spans become shorter. I have seen an expectation set that stimulation must be gained from an interaction with as little effort as possible. A moment is worthless if it does not contain intense stimulation. This, in turn, can stymie interest in meeting new people and making new connections. If you take a chance, you might not gain any stimulation from this connection. This risk that we take on each other is how relationships are formed. Those relationships can build and multiply into communities."
+ "All sounds have an effect" [Nicolás Jaar] [The Politics of Dancing] "Contemporary dance music, particularly in illegal raves, unites a few things that gives it potential for insurrectionary activity: an autonomous concentration of people moving in unison, claiming a space of land, safe spaces for sexual freedom, etc. For some, this is “revolutionary” enough because it shows a glimmer of potential agency “outside” of certain systems. Is it enough? Is anything ever enough? For some, nothing will be enough until the current world system of constant exploitation is made inoperative. I think I’m more in this camp, with all the contradictions that it entails as a touring musician who releases music on streaming platforms."
