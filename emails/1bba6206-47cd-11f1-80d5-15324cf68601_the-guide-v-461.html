+ Queer Happened Here +

+ Reade Street, located at 143 Reade Street in Tribeca, briefly operated from approx. 1974–1976 as Michael Brody’s 1st club & the foundation for what would become the legendary Paradise Garage. Housed in a 2-floor warehouse space, often described as a vault within an old furrier’s building, it functioned as a small, semi-underground dance floor. Reade Street became the 1st venue where Brody installed Levan as a resident DJ, building the night around the DJ–dancefloor relationship & outfitting the room with a serious sound system (Bozak mixer, McIntosh amps, Klipschorn speakers).

+ The Enchanted Garden was Steve Rubell & Ian Schrager’s 1st large-scale club experiment prior to Studio 54, where they 1st tested themed environments, extravagant lighting, & spectacle-driven programming. Opened in late 1975, it operated inside the clubhouse of the former North Hills Country Club, now known as Douglaston Manor at 63-20 Commonwealth Blvd in Queens.

+ By at least the late 1970s, Johnny’s Pub operated as a quiet but steady neighborhood gay bar & restaurant at 123 E. 47th Street between Lexington & Third Avenues in Manhattan’s Midtown East neighborhood. It would remain there until at least the early 1990s.

+ Tory’s, sometimes referred to as Tory’s World, operated as a gay nightclub & event space during the early 1980s at 119 E. 27th Street (between Park & Lex).

+ Pizza-A-Go-Go operated at 121 W. 31st Street during the early ‘80s as a small Midtown pizzeria repurposed at night into a makeshift party space.

+ At 380 Lafayette Street, under Time Cafe at Great Jones, Fez operated from 1992-2005 as one of downtown Manhattan’s most important small rooms: an intimate cabaret that could seat roughly 100 & which consistently mixed music, comedy, performance, & nightlife crowds.
