+ Palestine +

+ Impotence, Exhaustion and the Sinister Patience of Sónar [First Floor] "Sónar is still owned by Superstruct, which is owned by KKR, a private equity giant tied to resource extraction, weapons manufacturing, surveillance technology, and businesses that operate in the occupied West Bank. And yet, hundreds of artists (and their teams) signed on to play this year’s festival anyways."

"On a long enough timeline, pretty much anything—even complicity in genocide—can be normalized; what’s truly depressing, however, is that in the case of Sónar and nearly all of the events under the Superstruct / KKR umbrella, the necessary timeline was less than a year."

"In the face of powerful actors (like Sónar / Superstruct / KKR) and the sense that virtually everything and everyone is ethically compromised, even the most engaged agitants are often paralyzed by feelings of impotence."

+ On Sónar or the topology of impotence [Salto] "The collective benefits of individually forgoing certain practices are always vague, distant, and difficult to visualize. However, the personal costs of renunciation are perceived much more tangibly (missing a festival, not going on vacation, not benefiting from real estate speculation, giving up meat...)."

"The problem (and here lies the master trap of the topology of impotence) is that the dilemma is never solvable from an individual perspective. But the boycott is not an individual action, but a collective one."

"The only way for festivals to change is for the public to stop going. However much we believe we lack agency, individual decisions still carry weight, and change begins with a refusal that disrupts the status quo."

+ Why Boiler Room's Dublin Announcement Backfired [Midnight Rebels] "The comments filled with Palestinian flags, and within five minutes, the post vanished. I watched a massive media campaign get deleted in real-time because a room full of local kids refused to play along. When the corporate parent of your favorite party brand is KKR, the illusion of the underground collapses."
