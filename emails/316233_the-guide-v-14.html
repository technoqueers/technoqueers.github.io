(( Mix Of The Week // Volvox ))

For those of us still feeling depleted from last weekend’s 36-hour marathon, do not despair! Local hero Ariana Paoletti, a.k.a. Volvox, is here to remind us that raw, acid-tinged techno can be just what the doctor ordered to rouse you from your mid-week slumber.

In this recent mix for the Cologne-based label and collective Noorden, you’ll hear Volvox weave dark, EBM-influenced sounds together with her signature jackin’ acid house and techno to create an expansive, futuristic landscape of rhythm and dissonance. Its unrelenting pace and thick distortion simultaneously attracts and repels, yet a steady supply of acid-flavored grooves and industrial-grade hi-hats and snares will keep your head bobbing (or body moving) throughout.

This mix’s unapologetic intensity is no mistake. Yesterday we chatted with Ari at Bossa Nova Civic Club, where she holds a monthly residency, and she told us that she recorded this mix immediately following the events in Orlando. She stated that this mix acted as a form of catharsis, and allowed her to express the frustration, rage, and despair that she felt in that moment. Ultimately, she said, we can’t stop fighting for our connection to these spaces, and solidarity through music is a key motivator behind her nonstop DJ schedule.

You can catch Volvox this Friday at Collapse Factory, where she’ll be playing alongside locals R. Gamble, Antenes, Kimyon, SPRKLBB, and Kristine Barilli. This will be Ari’s last appearance in the U.S. before she heads to Europe with her Discwoman comrades for a two-month tour. 

So come out, jack your body, and give our favorite techno maven the send-off she deserves! RSVP for Collapse Factory via the TQ reduced list and you’ll be charged only $10 at the door.
