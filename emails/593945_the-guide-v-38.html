MIX OF THE WEEK // DEREK PLASLAIKO
Derek Plaslaiko is one of dance music’s humblest yet most respected selectors. As a former employee at Detroit’s storied Record Time and favorite of the mid-1990s Detroit warehouse circuit, the Michigan native developed a style characterized by high-octane vinyl mixing, extended sets, and deep musical knowledge. In 2004, Plaslaiko relocated to New York and met Bryan Kasenic, which began a decade-long residency at The Bunker and saw the local party turn into a world-renowned event.

Now based in Berlin, Derek holds a residency at Club der Visionaere and tours throughout Europe. However, he remains most beloved for his appearances at Detroit’s infamous No Way Back party, and travels frequently to cities like Pittsburgh, Chicago, and Seattle, to play extended sets in similarly intimate settings. As he told Sequencer magazine, “I have never been one to shy away from playing someplace just because its scene is ‘small’. In fact, I’m always looking for more cities that fit that description.”

Despite his high-profile bookings and packed schedule, it’s clear that Derek gets the most joy out of playing for close friends and family. Perhaps the best example of this is his legendary 12-hour Boiler Room set. Filmed in his living room, Derek delivers an eclectic, mind-bending set while his wife, 3 year-old son, and friends drift in and out of the frame and day slowly becomes night.

This week’s mix, recorded as the 88th installment of The Bunker’s podcast series, dives deep into heady techno, squelching acid, and jacking, stripped-back house. Moods build gradually while thundering bursts of impact arrive at just the right moment. The soundscape brims with dark, snaking basslines, banging drums, and playful 303-derived sounds.

Derek returns to New York this Saturday for The Bunker’s 14 Year Anniversary party, where he’ll play alongside old friends and fellow Detroit veterans Mike Servito and Patrick Russell. He’ll also be sharing the bill with Romans, Bryan Kasenic, Wrecked, Antenes, and Justin Cudmore. See you on the dancefloor!
