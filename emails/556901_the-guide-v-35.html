MIX OF THE WEEK // PEARSON SOUND

Pearson Sound, a.k.a Dan Kennedy, is a standard-bearer of U.K. dance music and one of its most thrilling innovators working today. A product of the fruitful mid-2000s British dubstep scene, Kennedy first emerged in 2006 producing amorphous, sub-bass-driven, and wildly syncopated tracks under the alias Ramadanman.

Over the next decade, Kennedy continued to create frenetic club bangers and darker, more experimental material alike, gradually paring down jungle and garage-derived elements to arrive at a more dance floor-oriented sound. Following the release of his beautifully abstract self-titled album in 2015, Kennedy turned his focus back to the club with XLB -- a raucous, effervescent EP that oscillates between disorientation and euphoria.

As co-head of respected label Hessle Audio, Kennedy is also responsible for over 20 of the U.K’s most essential 12-inches from the likes of Ben UFO, Pangea (both of whom co-run the label), Objekt, and himself.

This mix, recorded live at Dekmantel 2016, plunges headfirst into the depths of U.K. dance music. Throughout, Kennedy deftly layers afrobeats, breaks, and thundering basslines, mixing tracks at breakneck speed. Brimming with jagged energy, the experience is nothing less than pure, sweaty fun.

Catch Pearson Sound this Saturday at Good Room, where he’ll play alongside local favorite Anthony Naples all night. See you on the dance floor!
