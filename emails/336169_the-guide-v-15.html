Zine of the week: Discwoman presents Rave Ethics for Women

(( Mix Of The Week ))
(( Aurora Halal ))

All hail the multitalented producer, DJ, video artist, and dark princess of Brooklyn’s underground techno scene: Aurora Halal.

In this mix recorded for Static Discos’ Dimensions podcast series, Aurora oscillates between shadowy techno and dreamy deep-house, creating a hypnotic and emotive sonic landscape. While not overtly aimed at the dance floor, she deftly layers basslines, drums, and melodic textures to capture the highs and lows a DJ set ought to achieve. No beat is wasted, and the tracks she selects continually build on, evolve from, and morph into one another. This produces an all-encompassing, rolling feeling that carries the listener from start to finish on a gentle wave of rhythm.

Perhaps the most striking moment arrives near the halfway point, when hazy synth loops from Huerco S.’ “Untitled (Wicked Bass)” give way to an equally dreamy, unreleased Slava track recorded live at one of Aurora’s Mutual Dreaming parties. Amidst the squelching bassline and airy blanket of high-end sounds, a reverbed “Shante you stay” echoes siren-like in the background. RuPaul has never sounded so celestial.

Aurora shared with Static Discos her inspiration for this mix: “Seeing legends Terrence Dixon, DJ Sprinkles and Function in particular left me with new sense of dance music as elemental geometry balanced between the extremes of human emotion: ecstatic & heavenly, or twisted & frightening. This mix explores that space between – it goes from dark and churning to sensual, soft and introspective, and the various moods along the way.”

She returns to Brooklyn this Saturday to deliver a much-anticipated set alongside Discwoman label-boss Umfang, aboard Good Room’s “Techno Overboard” boat party. This will be Aurora’s last performance before appearing at Sustain-Release in September, do don’t sleep on this rare opportunity! <3
