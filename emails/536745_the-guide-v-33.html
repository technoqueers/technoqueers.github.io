Today is World AIDS Day, aka Ronald Reagan Ignored AIDS Until It Started Killing Straight White People Day

The unfathomable loss of life, of love, of creativity, of queer spirits that had so much more to give before their lives were cut short -- the magnitude of this loss means that even if we find a cure, the crisis is never truly "over".

Far from it: VP-elect Mike Pence once said he would redirect funding for HIV/AIDS initiatives to fund conversion therapy. We hope there is extra room inside Ronald Reagan's rusty, dangling cage in the flaming pits of hell.

If all of us freaks and queers and ravers were our same selves, only in the 80's or early 90's instead of now, we would be spending at least as much time at each other's funerals each weekend as we would on the dancefloor.

Why Clubbing Was Crucial for Gay Men During the AIDS Crisis by Steve Weinstein

"Beyond offering collective catharsis, dancefloors can be potent breeding grounds for political mobilization."

It's Time to Start Thinking About How Queer Nightlife Can Move Forward Under Trump by Michelle Lhooq, THUMP's Features Editor. She recently invited us to participate in her round table discussion re: The New Queer Dance Floor (hosted by VICE).

As for this Friday: no matter where you begin your journey, your final destination shall not change.

Speaking of Unter... here are dates for the next two, which you should certainly PrEP for:

1/27: Slav Squats Galore
3/3: The British Invasion
Thursday
Cultivated Sound presents: Cry Baby / Marite / Maŕa / rtmc / DVG @ TBA Brooklyn - 395 Wythe Ave // This lineup mostly comprised of local female artists spins techno til 4 AM, starting with happy hour, and ending with you "calling in sick" on Friday. Cry Baby, Marite, and Deanna Viva Garcia are joined by Cultivated Sound residents Maŕa and rtmc.
Friday
808 Presents Discwoman: Tama Sumo & Lakuti, Umfang & Analog Soul @ Sugar Hill Restaurant & Supper Club // The illustrious Berghain/Panorama Bar resident Tama Sumo plays alongside her wife Lakuti, herself a supreme selector and founder of Uzuri Recordings. They're joined by our own Umfang and Analog Soul, and we are extremely excited to let our freak flags fly on what promises to be a very queer dancefloor! Wristbands from this event = only $15 door at Unter, so join the exodus at 4 AM!
JACK DEPT. NYC // TX Connect / Volvox / John Barera @ Bossa // TX Connect, from JACK DEPT's own recently launched label, is joined by residents Volvox and John Barera. The non-ticketed option with an intimate feel, guaranteed to deliver til 4 AM.
The Bunker: Magda, T.B. Arthur, Mike Servito @ Analog BKNY // Magda, herself part of the Bunker family, headlines alongside her collaborator T.B. Arthur, a mysterious artist making his east coast debut and treating us to a live set. They're joined by Mike Servito and Bryan Kasenic, back from their travels to play on home turf. Immerse yourself in acid-laced techno til 6 AM!
UNTER x MORD feat. Bas Mooy, UVB, Volvox & Olga @ Bushwick Warehouse // Our favorite after hours techno mind fuck goes til 11 AM. Bas Mooy and UVB bring dark, industrial sounds, joined by Volvox and Olga. Retreat from the inferno to the ambient room, featuring DJ Sleepy Baby, Kiddo Sincere, and Ital. Remember to bring your sunglasses for when you eventually leave!
Saturday
Wrecked w/ Chris Cruse (Spotlight, LA) @ Analog BKNY // Club Toilet comes to Brooklyn! This is the sweaty gay boys option, with sweaty gay underground tunes to go with it, compliments of Chris Cruse. This underground function taking place in the most decadent of overground (yet still inaccessible) venues rages on til 7 AM.
Giorgio Gigli - US Debut, Shawn O'Sullivan, & Rose E. Kross @ The Gateway // Less messy, more intimate, but no less subdued will be the US debut of Giorgio Gigli, an Italian producer treating us to an hypnotic and atmospheric extended DJ set til 4 AM, sure to showcase his 'future tech noir' sound. He's joined by locals Shawn O'Sullivan (live) and Rose E. Kross. Since The Gateway's default sound and lighting is garbage (i.e. unfit for techno), the organizers are importing their own setup so these artists can really shine. Also -- if you miss Giorgio tonight, you can catch him this Monday at Bossa, where he'll be playing a live ambient set, joined by Clay Wilson b2b Certain Creatures.
Convextion [LIVE], Antenes, Alex from Queens @ Trans-Pecos // This event also features a rare artist playing for an intimate dancefloor til 4 AM, so it might boil down to whether you're more excited to see Georgio Gigli or Convextion. Convextion (aka E.R.P. when producing electro) is a dub techno producer whose work has been sporadic yet highly impactful. His Detroit-influenced sound can be described as deep, futuristic, and even melancholic. He's joined by TQ favorite Antenes and Alex from Queens.
