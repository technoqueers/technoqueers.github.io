+ Sex, Clubs, Dissent charts an expansive visual history of queer nightlife through the lens of photographers, filmmakers, and artists. The book explores how image-making has fostered, and at times jeopardised, the formation of queer practices, subcultures, and forms of resistance. + Opened in 1939, Lenox Lounge operated for over 7 decades as a Harlem jazz & blues institution at 288 Lenox Ave/Malcolm X Blvd, known for its Art Deco interior, zebra-print banquettes, & late-night crowd of musicians, writers, & neighborhood regulars–which included James Baldwin, Langston Hughes, & even Madonna, who shot her music video for “Secret” there. Most notably, the bar is linked in Harlem jazz lore to Billie Holiday, who had a favored or “reserved” booth to her name. […] The historic building was demolished in 2017, a loss emblematic of Harlem’s cultural displacement. [Queer Happened Here]
+ Operating in the late 1970s & early ‘80s, Peeches was an Upper East Side women’s/lesbian bar & disco at 1201 Lexington Avenue & E. 81st Street. At the time, it was part of a small but vital network of lesbian nightlife spaces in Upper Manhattan, which included venues like Dapper & Friends & Pat Hemmingway’s Bull’s Head. [QHH]
+ Starlight Bar & Lounge operated at 167 Avenue A (at E. 11th Street) from 1998 to 2008 as a mixed lesbian/gay  venue owned by longtime producer Wanda Acosta (Wonderbar, Clubhouse, Cafe Tabac, etc), Jon Johnson & Jack Hazan. Conceived as a glam, lounge-driven alternative to the East Village dive model, Starlight featured moody lighting, plush banquettes, an active central bar, & a back room with wall-to-wall seating & a small stage. […] On Sundays, men were welcome but required to be accompanied by a woman. [QHH]
