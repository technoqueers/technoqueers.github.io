+ Gentrification +

+ Meet Me in the Darkroom [n+1] "Mindlessly hedonistic, snobbishly insular, and punishingly cliquish though it might seem (and is), the booming Bushwick club scene has lately drawn the attention of “serious” writers and thinkers."

"Nightclubs are businesses, and real estate is expensive, and so many of the enterprising souls that turn deindustrialized wastes into cultural capital are, well, cishet white guys with trust funds or business partners, who crave underground cachet."

"Some clubs even hire bathroom attendants who dutifully hand out napkins and candies to G’ed-out creative directors and project managers."

"These party scenes are both enabled and imperiled by the forces of gentrification. While underground nightlife spaces are often heavily trans and queer, they remain mostly white, in a famously majority-minority metropolis."

"Unlike many parties that I frequent, Soul Summit is a majority-black function, featuring people who are actually there to dance, and dance well."

"I don’t want people who resemble the most heinous characters on White Lotus to be at Basement while I’m snorting coke in a jockstrap and getting railed and bred against a wall."

+ An Interview With a DJ Who Was Supposed to Play Ginger's Before Noise Complaints Shut Down Pride Events [Hell Gate] "Ginger's installed soundproofing when they shut down in 2021. So I'm so curious why it wasn't enough. [...] I hope that something coalesces that's a little bit of a counter to the cancellation. Maybe something like a scream-in: You pay $5 to scream for five minutes, and then that pays for the soundproofing."

+ Chemsex & Dating Under Capitalism +

+ Party and play: chemsex in gay dating culture by The Ashtray [Technomaterialism] "The logic of capitalism shapes interpersonal relationships, making them more transactional; this leads to more competitiveness and performance pressure, and chemsex as a coping mechanism."

+ 'Hot white gays' having difficulty dating is a symptom of capitalism [@nathanramospark] "Because conventionally attractive white gays grew up benefitting from capitalism and white supremacy, they've been raised with this notion that connection is about power. How much capital can you have? How good looking can you be? How many connections do you have? What is your job? When they're at the bar, or the warehouse part, or on the apps, they're programmed to see hierarchy. When you're programmed to only see hierarchy, and everyone that you're into is only programmed to see hierarchy, you're all kind of doomed to look past each other." [#reels]
