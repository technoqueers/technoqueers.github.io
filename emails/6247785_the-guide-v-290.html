Before Stonewall Inn there was Julius’ — now an official city landmark

By Olivia Ebertz, Gothamist

In 1966, three years before the Stonewall riots, a gay rights group used the location to challenge regulations by the State Liquor Authority requiring bartenders to refuse service to gay people. Members of the Mattachine Society, the country’s largest gay rights group at the time, held a protest event at the West Village bar they called a "sip-in."
