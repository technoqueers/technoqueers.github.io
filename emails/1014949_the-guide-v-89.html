Eris Drew announces Motherbeat series of all-night parties in the US // "The deep ecstatic psychedelic roots of the scene appear to be subjugated to other values. I want to put those upfront, so the time seems right for a crazy trans woman and her friends [to] inject a little chaos, femme energy and psychedelic love into the mix."
Fri 6 Apr // the 1st Psychedelic Rite of the Motherbeat: Shamaness Imploding

RA. 604 Eris Drew // The records I play for people today, a lot of them were my medicine. Before transition, I would evoke the archetype of the goddess in private and feel connected with my feminine-self through music, dance and adornment. She was my only escape from gender dysphoria and oppression for two decades.

How to keep a party going for 15 years, with Bryan Kasenic // 15 years later, the landscape is completely different. There are a few established legal club venues in NYC who are hosting interesting artists every weekend, and one-offs happening all over the place. Every weekend in NYC these days, there are many parties for people to choose from, of all shapes and sizes. The scene in general in the US has also grown by leaps and bounds.

Forty years of sex and gender in the wilds of New York // The neighborhood is now thoroughly gentrified and unaffordable to ordinary people, and the aesthetic, which was fundamentally countercultural, has become entirely domesticated in the professional art world. Two exhibitions on view in New York seem to bookend not just the heyday of the East Village and its ultimate dissolution into the luxury real estate economy, but the transmigration of the aesthetic itself, its birth, death and reincarnation in a zombified, academic form.
