(( artist of the week ))
(( Terre Thaemlitz aka DJ Sprinkles ))

“The house nation likes to pretend clubs are an oasis from suffering, but suffering is in here, with us."

There is perhaps no artist more central in bringing issues of queerness to the forefront of dance music than Terre Thaemlitz, a.k.a, DJ Sprinkles, a.k.a. K-S.H.E, a.k.a. G.R.R.L., a.k.a. Terre’s Neu Wuss Fusion. Through 15 solo albums, numerous 12-inch singles and remixes, countless essays and writings, and various multimedia projects, Thaemlitz has sought to remind us that house music was borne of struggle: a specific queer, black/latino, and transgender struggle. Her immense body of work focuses a critical lens on identity politics, commercial media production, labor exploitation, and transgender communities  -- all articulated with a brilliantly lucid, unmistakable voice.

Thaemlitz moved to New York from rural Missouri in the mid-1980s to study fine art, but quickly immersed himself in the city’s queer house scene. By the early 1990s, he (Terre uses both male and female pronouns) became a resident at Sally’s II, a club formerly located on W 43rd Street that catered mainly to trans sex workers, which shaped both her personal outlook and DJing style. For the past 15 years she has lived in Japan, where she has owned and operated her own fiercely independent label, Comatonse Recordings, that has released the bulk of her work.

Each project Terre undertakes centers around a particular theme. The 2003 electro-acoustic-ambient album “Lovebomb”, for instance, is a searing critique of "Love" as it has been manipulated by the audio marketplace. 2012’s Soulnessless, by contrast, is the world's first “full-length mp3 album”, and its audio and visual components run for roughly 30 hours, serving as “both an attempt to explore the unprecedented recording lengths offered by the MP3 format, and as an act of resistance to online distributorships", Thaemlitz told in RA in 2012. 2011’s reissued Routes Not Roots features stories of transgender experience told in monologues over hypnotic deep-house tracks, suggesting that rather than search for the roots of transgenderism, biological or otherwise, we’d be better off attending to the experiences of particular people—to their “routes.”

Ultimately, what Thaemlitz touches upon in all her work is queerness, a feeling characterized by the interplay of pleasure and shame. She forces us to acknowledge that this process is not universal, cannot be branded as PrideTM and easily spread by the HRC, but rather is informed by suffering and realized through inescapable power dynamics. It is tied to specific places, races, and socioeconomic groups -- much like house music.

“House isn't so much a sound as a situation.” The first words spoken on the intro track of Midtown 120 Blues, DJ Sprinkles’ seminal 2009 album, encapsulate the theme at the heart of Terre’s most celebrated work. Presented as a reflection of 1980s and early 1990s queer underground spaces in New York City, the album is an immersive and revelatory experience. Volumes can be said of the record, but consider the final lines of the intro track monologue as a critical reminder to all of us who participate in underground dance music:
 

“So what was the New York house sound? House wasn't so much a sound as a situation. The majority of DJ's - DJ's like myself - were nobody's in nowhere clubs: unheard and unpaid. In the words of Sylvester: reality was less ‘everybody is a star,’ and more ‘I who have nothing.’

Twenty years later, major distribution gives us Classic House, the same way soundtracks in Vietnam war films gave us Classic Rock. The contexts from which the Deep House sound emerged are forgotten: sexual and gender crises, transgendered sex work, black market hormones, drug and alcohol addiction, loneliness, racism, HIV, ACT-UP, Tompkins Sq. Park, police brutality, queer-bashing, underpayment, unemployment and censorship - all at 120 beats per minute.”

*** 

Given Terre’s contribution to queer discourse, it can be easy to forget that she is a remarkably talented DJ and producer. Her style of deep house favors long, drawn-out grooves that wax and wane, constructed from subdued, propulsive drum loops, muted instrumentals, and mesmerizing vocal samples. Her use of sampling is particularly unique, as she views samples as “footnotes”, and uses them as “ways of connecting and constructing a deep and rich history around the music that we're making” as she told RA.

The best way to appreciate Terre’s oeuvre is to dive in head first. To this end, we’ve compiled a list of our favorite Terre Thaemlitz interviews, albums, mixes, and projects for you to explore. This is by no means comprehensive, but nonetheless a starting point.

Quest, Reade Truth + more // NY techno pioneer Reade Truth, known for delivering eclectic and innovative sets, is joined by Quest, Velasco, and Taylor Shockley. Hosted at a secret warehouse location til 9 AM.
Optimo All Night Long + Lena Willikens and Willie Burns in the Bad Room // An evening of leftfield techno selections from Optimo (Twitch and Jonnie Wilkes) and Lena Willikens, joined by resident and WT Records founder Willie Burns.
Body Language with JD Samson & Whitney Day // Not in the mood for techno? JD Samson and Whitney Day will be igniting this radically-inclusive, femme-centric dancefloor with disco, funk, house, hip-hop, and more!

French Kiss presents DJ Sprinkles with DJ Aakmael and Douglas Sherman in the Bad Room // A rare chance to catch the inimitable DJ Sprinkes, a truly prolific DJ, producer, queer theorist, and hero of our movement to reclaim the dance floor. She's joined by DJ Aakmael. Expect lots of deep house!
Twice as Proper - Ceremony with Electric Indigo (Indigo:Inc, Female:Pressure) // Techno legend and Female:Pressure founder Electric Indigo headlines this edition of Twice As Proper, joined by Craft, Headliner, and Shadi.

Planet E | Carl Craig pres Modular Pursuits/ Octave One/ Luke Hess at Output and Kenny Larkin/ Recloose/ Jay Daniel in The Panther Room // The prolific Carl Craig plugs his synths and drum machines into Output's Funktion-One sound system, performing a rare live set as Modular Pursuits. Basement Floor Festivus: Speculator + Entro Senestre // Local techno innovators Speculator (aka Willie Burns, WT Records) and Entro Senestre spearhead this edition of Basement Floor, joined by resident Chung.
808 Presents Area10 | MK, Omar S, CamelPhat,Turtle Bugg // Throw on your tracksuit for this night of serious, sweaty dancing at Sugar Hill Disco! House tunes delivered by MK, Omar-S, CamelPhat, and Turtle Bugg.
