UV Podcast 052 & interview with Konkrit // Every word that today is being said about the underground and thus local and independent scenes instantly sounds to us like hollow PR dreck even if it’s idealistic satire, because hollow PR dreck tries to emulate idealism whenever it can. Yes, I think it is absolutely desirable to (re-)build local scenes and, more importantly, new infrastructures.

But even before that we have to scheme, plot and develop strategies in order to make those words and the values they are meant to promote immune or at least resilient towards

a) the fast turnover of the scene’s attention economy – especially in social media-based communication – and

b) any attempts of co-optation, whether by global corporations or Ibiza-based Tech House dudes.

Social media is negatively affecting DJs' mental health // Women and minority groups within the industry are most likely to cop abuse within dance music, as they are generally online. And as everyone’s plugged into social media 24/7, DJs invariably see all the shitty stuff that’s being said about them—or even sent to them directly. After Resident Advisor got rid of their DJ poll in 2017, The Black Madonna called on YouTube to delete their comment section. “It’s damaging,” she wrote in a Twitter thread. “I straight up went to therapy over this shit. It was like being whacked with a shovel.”


This New Brooklyn Dance Party Celebrates the Black and Queer Roots of House Music // “When you say deejaying to most people, they think of confetti cannons and people jumping up and down, and lights and glow sticks,” Chicago-based house icon and Smart Bar resident DJ Heather explains. "That's why we have celebrations about Frankie Knuckles, his birthdays, his foundations. Letting people know about those origins. We talk about Chicago house, about different clubs . . . New York stories are very different than Chicago stories, but again the commonality is in youth culture, [in] brown, gay, dance culture. It has to be part of the history that people have to be aware of."
