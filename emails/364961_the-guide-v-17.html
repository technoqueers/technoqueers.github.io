Techno Queers NY

(( Mix Of The Week & Artist Profile ))

(( Patrick Russell ))

Patrick Russell is no stranger to long-form DJ sets nor is he a newcomer to Brooklyn dancefloors. The Detroit-bred Bunker resident, perhaps best known these days for his yearly appearances at Movement’s beloved No Way Back party, has been hard at work innovating within the boundaries of minimal, machine-driven techno for several decades. As a product of Detroit’s early 90s techno scene, where he played alongside the likes of Mike Servito and Derek Plaslaiko, Patrick Russell has refined a style of DJing that centers on creating deep, hypnotic grooves through a blend of acid, IDM, techno, electro and left-field selections -- all stitched together seamlessly by a virtuosic mastery of the decks.

Back in February, Russell closed out Bunker x Unter’s first 36-hour party with an epic 10-hour set. He put forth a first-hand account of the experience in Thump, where he detailed his approach to marathon sets:

“It's kind of cliché to say that a DJ set should take people on a journey. A lot of people say that, but just because you're playing some house and techno doesn't mean you're taking the dancefloor on a journey. I like telling stories, getting from one point to another over a long period of time, using interesting records that are maybe difficult to mix, and creating this weird dialogue with the crowd. I challenge myself pretty intensely, and while it is incredibly stressful, it's ten times more rewarding."

This mix, recorded for Bunker’s podcast series, clocks in at an uncharacteristically short 57 minutes -- but don’t let the length fool you. Russell is a sonic storyteller through and through, and from the thud of the first kick, a ripple of muted strings immediately plunges the listener under a canopy of richly textured sounds and polyrhythms. Glimmering bleeps and tight hi-hats slowly crystallize into hypnotic loops, folding into themselves and sputter forward with endless tiny variations. Hissing distortion overlays darkly warped melodies and slowly crescendoes towards some unknown point only to reveal a blistering acid line lurking just below the surface. Cosmic blips and jitters fade in and out strobe-like, condensing the momentum into a ball of white-hot energy. A mechanized voice suddenly materializes, speaking of chemical warfare and “organo-phosphates.” Out of this chaos, a dusty, burned-out groove emerges to quietly release the tension and round out the mix.

Russell’s style of techno, like any minimalist aesthetic, uses restraint and negative space to create striking contrasts. Slow, intricately woven buildups -- hours long in some instances, provide the foundation for devastating moments of impact. What is unique about Patrick Russell is that he doesn’t just view this approach as a way to maximize dancefloor energy, but rather as a means to tap into something more fundamental. In 2010, he told the blog Don’t Forget to Go Home:

“Take techno and house for example: If you collectively boil down the recorded history of these genres to their most concentrated and deepest essence, could you really verbally define what that is, exactly? I think most of us have felt it at least once, yet we all have different and arbitrary man-made words for it. A cross between haunted memory, elevation to an out-of-body experience, a temporal shift, primal sexuality, the unstoppable desire to go all tasmanian devil on a dancefloor? I'm not referring to generic get-wasted party music here but the occasional peek we get into something much deeper, violently burned to the roots and free of any trendiness, posturing, or gimmicks.”

The bona fide renaissance man that he is, Patrick will be spinning disco at Black Flamingo’s Capriccio Disco Version party this Saturday. Come for the warm grooves and show your support for one of New York’s unsung dancefloor heroes!

Tropical Goth: Analog Soul (Dat Kat & Jacky Sommer), TK & Cherry 2000, Kiddo Sincere, Chris Video // Twin sister DJ duo Analog Soul (aka Jacky Sommer & DatKat) will take you on a ride through house, Detroit techno, acid, dub, electronica, and more, joined by TK & Cherry 2000, Kiddo Sincere, and Chris Video.
Friday
Industry of Machines: Mick Wills, C10, Rose E Kross, AxFx // German underground acid, techno and darkwave legend Mick Wills is here to turn Bossa's dance floor into a fogged-out vortex from which there is no escape. He's joined by C10, a Baltimore-based underground DJ, live performer, and producer who will bathe you in darker shades of experimental, atmospheric techno. Rose E Kross will be driving the dancefloor with raw techno and industrial tracks across genres past and present. Bring your favorite all-black getup to compliment the vibe and to hide how much you'll be sweating it out!
Saturday
BLIND Techno Mary Yuzosvskaya, Joe Dismal, Spin D // Venture off to the basement of Ridgewood DIY venue Trans-Pecos for the first of a new series of pure techno parties from Blind Colors NY. Local underground favorite Mary Yuzovskaya will mesmerize you with her vinyl selection and superior mixing technique. She's joined by NY native Spin D, a master of the darker side of techno who is also known for curating only the finest techno parties. Also on board is Joe Dismal, a DJ from Texas who lights up dance floors with his eclectic techno sets.
 
Childhood Industries: Milton Bradley / Young Male / Ciarra Black / Karl Ewald / Witness // East Berliner Milton Bradley, affiliated with Dystopian and Henning Baer's Grounded Theory Berlin, explores many genres through his various aliases. Tonight he will be making you move to some trippy, dubbed-out techno. White Material co-founder, DJ, and producer Young Male (who has played both Berghain main room and Panorama Bar) promises to deliver a genre-itinerant set powered by sleek techno. Completing the lineup is Ciarra Black, who will ignite the dance floor with her live sets exploring the spaces between noise and techno.
Capriccio ★Disco Version★ w/ Patrick Russell & Alex from Queens at Black Flamingo // Local underground veteran Patrick Russell, a genuine sonic storyteller, offers up a rare disco set at this special edition of Capriccio. He magically synthesizes the diaspora of acid, IDM, and pure jack energy into a deep, musical dialog that always makes the dance floor descend into a hypnotic, primal state of mind. He is joined by Alex from Queens, another local underground mainstay sporting a vast vinyl selection. He will transport you back to those disco, house, funky, and raw NY days, so bring colorful attire and dress to sweat! Free before midnight.
