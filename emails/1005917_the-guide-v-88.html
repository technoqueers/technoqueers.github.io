VRAAA 2017 Diva Rankings // It’s the time of the year again! The time to know who’s who of the biggest DJ divas around. With the extinction of Resident Advisor’s top DJ ranking, I dare to say this is the most relevant underground DJ list around. Unlike them, my lists were never dominated by straight white male European DJs (nothing wrong with them, tho… they are cute and fun to make out with); so I don’t feel the need to stop doing it. Neither the music nor the skills from the DJs in this list will be relevant here but the most important trait being judged is their sassiness, nerve, attitude and charisma!

15 industry figures tell us how to combat sexism and harassment in dance music // 
The Black Madonna // Having women in your life will help keep you honest.
Kim Ann Foxman // Just because you’re booking someone, you still could be doing more.
Nabihah Iqbal // It’s the attitudes of people that need to change rather than what we see at face value.
Jackmaster // speak up rather than just politely smiling or changing the subject when your mate makes an off-side joke.
Andrea Magdalina // We recently launched a mentorship programme, she.grows
Dan Beaumont // If you go to a venue and 50 per cent of the people don’t know why they’re there then you have a problem
Judy Griffith // We’ve got a welfare team now who, apart from checking that people are OK, also check for people in distress.
Toby Donnelly // the reality is that women are not in the more senior roles in the industry overall.
Amy Lamé // In July 2017 I held the first ever Women’s Night Safety summit with 100 change-makers, activists, the police, deputy mayors and more.
Tia Simon Campbell & Nadine Davis // Because we’re creating a conversation around what we’re doing, our party polices itself.
Gabriel Szatan // we need to show them the way to be a healthier part of dance music culture.
Haai // It would be so much more helpful to educate people before they step inside the club.
Melissa Taylor // lack of awareness of all the incredible women who’ve been part of the industry for years but were ignored by the media.
Sky Deep // There needs to be some kind of mandate where there’s more women, more brown and more queer people in security
Andy George // We’ve set-up a live phone number for anyone to call at any time

24/7 mental health service launched for music industry professionals // Help Musicians UK has established a round-the-clock helpline and a range of other services as a result of an academic study commissioned by the charity.
