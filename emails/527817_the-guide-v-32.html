(( mix of the week ))
(( Veronica Vasicka ))

Brooklyn-based DJ and Minimal Wave label boss Veronica Vasicka knows a thing or two about dark, synth-driven music. Sometimes described as a “vinyl archaeologist”, Vasicka grew up in New York in the 80s and early 90s sneaking into downtown clubs and digging obsessively though cold wave, italo disco, and experimental synth records of all kinds. She began her own label Minimal Wave in 2005 as a means of unearthing these forgotten classics and giving them second lives amongst today’s numerous synth-loving fans of dark, danceable electronic music. Much like Honey Soundsystem’s re-release of Patrick Cowley’s “Kickin’ in” relaunched interest in his career, Vasicka has done the same for artists like Oppenheimer Analysis -- whose lead singer Andy Oppenheimer (now 57) worked as an independent consultant on nuclear weapons before the reissue of his record, and now tours throughout Europe regularly.

 

As a DJ, Vasicka’s style is largely influenced by the types of records she releases on Minimal Wave, but also incorporates techno and industrial sounds more suited to a dancefloor. She has stated, “The ultimate success is to incorporate the influences of the past into something and take it beyond, take it one step further.”

 

This set, recorded live at Dekmantel 2016, is a delightfully dark, exhilarating 56-minute journey. Vasicka does not waste a second in creating immense waves of momentum that build and crest over the listener in repeating cycles. Bolstered by both heady techno loops and thick, scraping industrial and EBM tracks, Vasicka strikes a mesmerizing balance between carefully-measured techno elegance and basement-rave hedonism. The end result is spellbinding.

 

Catch her this Saturday at Schimanski (formerly Verboten), where she’ll be playing alongside TQ favorite Volvox and Delta Funktionen. If you’re in town, come find us on the dance floor!
