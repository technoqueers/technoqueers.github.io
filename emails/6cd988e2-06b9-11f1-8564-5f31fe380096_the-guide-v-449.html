+ Why is 2016 haunting 2026? On Postnostalgia, time loops and lost futures [Dazed] “Postnostalgia: a cultural and emotional state where the past perpetually resurfaces, repeating itself until it loses meaning, stripped of direction or consolation. Jacques Derrida described hauntology as the way the past continues to “haunt” the present, not as straightforward memory, but as a ghostly, unresolved presence. Decades later, Mark Fisher expanded this idea, observing that contemporary culture is haunted not just by what has been lost, but by futures that have never arrived. In a world shaped by acceleration and rising precarity, where the future feels inaccessible, we cling to the repeatable forms of memory.”
