(( artist of the week ))
(( DJ Bone ))

"(Underground) is basically being able to choose for yourself what you enjoy, not having it forced on you, not being fooled or swindled into believing something. Underground has talent. Underground can move in mysterious ways and still get the job done, even more so than someone (in the mainstream).  It's covert...not to be seen, it's not for everyone to put a face on it and market to the masses. Being in a magazine every month, every week ... and being just "entertainment" and not being there for a purpose ... that's not underground.” (Opening statement, Dj-bone.com)

Eric Dulan, a.k.a. DJ Bone, has earned himself a cult following over his twenty-year career by rejecting dance music trends to cultivate a style grounded in the art of DJing. He famously uses three or four decks during his sets, works the crossfader like a madman, and is unrelenting in his use of driving, Detroit-focused techno. This is someone who once compared digital-only DJs to chefs who cook only with a microwave, and for many years turned down lucrative DJ gigs to spend time raising his daughter. Bone’s approach is unconventional and uncompromising, but his utter dedication to playing no-frills, high-quality techno with unrivaled precision places him amongst the ranks of fellow Detroit luminaries Jeff Mills, Theo Parrish, and Kenny Dixon.

This mix, recorded for Rinse FM, pulses, thuds, and thunders, showcasing a breathtaking variety of techno styles and tempos in the process. It’s divided into two parts, with the first hour devoted to “techno you can feel” and the second to “techno you can dance to.” The combination is a masterclass in seamless mixing and underscores Bone’s ability to create ultra-deep, powerful grooves that clang and rumble, grooves that pull you in and refuse to let go.

Catch DJ Bone this Saturday alongside local favorite Aurora Halal at Trans-Pecos. See you on the dance floor!
