MIX OF THE WEEK // Jackie House


Jackie House, (a.k.a. Jacob Sperber), is ¼ of SF-based collective Honey Soundsystem and a musical juggernaut with a relentless passion for queer dance music and culture. Together with Ken Woodward, Sperber began Honey Soundsystem in 2007 in the Bay Area with the goal of reconnecting queer audiences to their dancefloor roots. Speaking with Red Bull Music Academy last year, Sperber stated, “One of the things that brought us all together is an archivist, historian sensibility. We’re trying to infuse that into every dancefloor, every party, every project.” Over time, Honey’s reputation grew due to their top quality bookings, sex-positive atmosphere, and bacchanalian party vibes. Now joined by Jason Kendig, Josh Cheon, and Robert Yang, Honey throw and DJ at some of the most celebrated parties in the global underground gay dance music circuit.

In this mix, released as the 36th installment of Pittsburgh-based Honcho’s podcast series, Jackie goes for the jugular and does not let go. Rollicking, machine-driven techno propels the mix forward, while punchy acid lines and dark electro-flavored bleeps and pops are woven throughout to keep the listener utterly transfixed. Like Jackie, the mix is unapologetically rowdy and a damn good time.

Catch Jackie play alongside the Wrecked boys this Saturday at Analog BKNY. Don’t miss out on what’s bound to be one for the books.
