+ Founded in 1968, the Nine Plus Club operated as a private, after-hours leather club rooted in NYC’s pre- & post-Stonewall gay motorcycle & leather scene. Its name was both literal & salaciously coded: “Nine” referred to the nine founding members; “Plus” acknowledged later members–& together, unofficially, the words signaled the club’s erotic bravado. Membership was both selective & sponsor-based, with formal rules governing conduct, guests, & initiation. [Queer Happened Here]
+ Active by the early 1970s & remembered as one of the borough’s most important early LGBTQ+ nightlife spaces outside Manhattan, The Alley was a neighborhood gay bar, dance club & drag performance space located at 74-05 37th Avenue in Jackson Heights, Queens. The Alley regularly featured female-impersonation performances (drag), anchoring its reputation as a performance bar as much as a dance spot. [QHH]
+ Located at 324 E. 49th Street (between First & Second Aves), Ronnie’s Supper Club was a Midtown Manhattan supper club & nightlife venue during the late 1960s–early 1970s, appearing consistently in gay press listings as a destination catering primarily to gay men. [QHH]
+ Throughout the late 1960s & 1970s, the minimalistically named Sauna Bath & Health Club (sometimes even just listed as the Sauna Baths) was a bathhouse frequented by gay, bisexual, & curious men at 300 W. 58th Street in Midtown Manhattan. [QHH]
+ In the late 70s, Emerald City was the first-ever TV show for the gays [Queerty] "The Emerald City aired twice weekly on Channel J where presenters would report on queer culture and politics with performance segments interspersed throughout. On-the-ground reporting took viewers into the biggest gay hotspots of the time, filming everywhere from Pride marches and Fire Island to legendary nightclub Reno Sweeney. […] The Emerald City was “Always determined to bring you the naked truth” (often quite literally in those bathhouse segments)."
