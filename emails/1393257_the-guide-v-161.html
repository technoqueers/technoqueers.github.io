Shout out to everyone who replied last week to ask that we keep the flyers, and that we do a bit of curation. It just didn't feel right without the flyers! Now we'll include them for some of our favorite events. If you have feedback for us, just reply to our emails! ☻
Melting Point is the NYC rave crew supporting immigrant rights // “In a moment where politics are so easily commodified in art and music” observes frequent Melting Point DJ Ashe Kilbourne, “it's exciting to see an event turning away from virtue signaling towards material support for activism”. While many parties announce their politics on their Facebook event page, Eric underlines that Melting Point seeks to “reclaim nightlife as a more focused space for political resistance and action”.

German McCarthyism // When the queer-feminist group Room 4 Resistance shared the #DJsForPalestine picture on their Facebook page in September 2018, they got an immediate reaction from the Berlin club About Blank. About Blank canceled an upcoming party the collective had announced for three days later and left them stranded with the expenses already incurred.

73 percent of independent musicians say they struggle with mental health // The report finds that 73 percent of independent musicians—as in, artists not signed to one of the three major labels (Universal, Sony and Warner)—are suffering with mental illness, which is defined here as experiencing "negative emotions such as stress, anxiety and/or depression in relation to their music creation." That number is even higher among artists who are 18-25 years old, with 80 percent reporting that they struggle with mental health issues.

Why patten have launched an old-school forum // Last week, patten opened the 555-5555 forum, an old-school space for discussing music outside of the social media bubble. It’s organised, properly archived and free of pervasive algorithms.

Is New York Finally Getting the Techno Club It Deserves? // In the past couple years, scores of new music venues and bars have been springing up all over the city; at the same time, many cherished DIY spaces have been forced to close. New York nightlife has therefore moved almost completely into legally sanctioned venues.

BASEMENT opening in New York // $7 Modelos and a surprisingly intense security presence meant that the club didn't totally nail the Berghain-style vibe it was going for. Overall, its dedication to a non-judgemental aesthetic, with low lighting and plenty of nooks and crannies to explore on and off the dance floor, felt like an improvement on most other legal spaces in New York.

I watched a bartender at the new techno club in NY refuse to give someone tap water in the cup they already had if they didn't pay $5 for a water bottle... when will clubs stop doing this...

Normies by day, fetish obsessives by night // The latex-clad patrons of Bound. During the day, they’re office regulars working a 9 to 5. At night, the kink and fetish gear comes out.

Susanne Bartsch, Fecal Matter, and a bunch of aliens take over Brooklyn // At one point, the evening’s guests were asked to hold off on recording or taking pictures. What followed was a performance, that simply put, was a handful to watch. Bartsch knows a thing or two about parties; after all, she’s been throwing them for three decades.

Generic Version of HIV Prevention Drug Truvada Coming in 2020 // Activist groups celebrate news, but warn PrEP drug could still be prohibitively expensive. While a month's supply could cost as little as $70 in some parts of the world, in the U.S. it could cost between $1,600 and $2,000.

A Million Americans Need This Drug. Trump’s Deal Won’t Help Enough of Them. // A donation of H.I.V.-prevention drugs from the pharmaceutical giant Gilead could benefit shareholders more than patients.

Andy Velez, a seminal New York AIDS and Latino Queer activist dies at 80 // He was involved in high-profile demonstrations and civil disobedience arrest scenarios that showcased ACT UP’s signature street theatre activism, such as chaining himself in the office of a pharmaceutical company, or covering himself in fake blood to symbolize the lives lost to AIDS because of government negligence.
