The State of Dance - The Week New York Lost Two Giants // From the outside, it would seem like New York’s dance music scene is healthier than ever. Clubs are often packed, with lines down the block on Fridays and Saturdays. Purchasing tickets ahead of time to go dance is sometimes mandatory, which itself isn’t problematic, though the prices are often prohibitive (blame DJ and agency fees, rent, greed — take your pick). But the reality is, while there are more people than ever that want to go dance, their tastes are just a susceptible to trends as they ever were. The economics of sustaining a business, or sustaining on your art in the dance-industrial complex just doesn’t add up, even at the highest level.

There are some beautiful examples in Nowadays, Sutherland, Black Flamingo, Bossa Nova, Mood Ring — and parties like Papi Juice, Soul Summit, 718 Sessions, whose community-centered efforts focus on their dancers and the music. Also, historically marginalized participants are setting more of the agenda, where not long ago they would have been excluded. There is a very strong industry-lead advocacy for inclusion on all levels, though club ownership consists of nearly nearly all white men (including Cielo and Output).

Dear Frankie: Advice on fuckbois, navigating singledom and the bro club // I believe being single is a place of resistance, I think often people just end up accepting the person they’re dating because they’re scared of being alone or not being loved. The commitment to this aspiration has damaging effects on your confidence and ambition. Every man I’ve dated has been a fuckboi, some more likeable than others. I really don’t think there are many men that aren’t. I don’t mean this in a bitter way but that it sociologically makes sense that they would be. They’ve pretty much been doing whatever they want with us forever and now we’re seeing a pretty big discourse and pushback around this but they just can’t keep up so they always kind of fail in a way.
