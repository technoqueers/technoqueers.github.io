(( Discodromo ))

Italian duo Discodromo, comprised of Giacomo Garavelloni and Giovanni Turco, are probably best known for their infamous CockTail D’Amore parties in Berlin. The pair created the monthly event in 2009 with Berghain-resident Boris, and in the years since, CockTail has become a much-loved fixture of Berlin’s underground gay scene due to its emphasis on free expression and high-quality music. While the party’s location has changed frequently over the years, or as Giacomo recently put it for Thump, they were “running from the cops, running from gentrification...going for years from one illegal venue to the next, until it was either shut down or destroyed”, CockTail’s center of gravity has always been Discodromo’s epic, time-bending DJ sets.

Stylistically, Giacomo and Giovanni favor slower tempos and emotive, synth-driven house with heavy nods to Italo, Electro, New Wave, and eclectic material of all sorts. As producers, the pair have released similarly deep, dark tracks on labels such as Phonica, Prins Thomas’ Internasjonal, and their own CockTail D’Amore Music. The name “Discodromo” is in fact the Italian word for the circular runway UFOs used in 1970s sci-fi films, which is apt considering their productions often possess a cosmic, dream-like quality.

This mix, recorded live at Panorama Bar in 2013, is unmistakably Discodromo’s handywork. Over the course of roughly four hours, the pair gracefully blend spacey Italo, foot-stomping disco, balearic synths, tribal grooves, and steely techno -- all the while maintaining a seamless, unbroken groove. It’s this careful attention to pacing and flow that makes it so easy to slip into a Discodromo set and lose track of time and self completely.

Catch Discodromo this Saturday at the Wrecked Halloween party, where they’ll be serving an extra spooky set alongside the Wrecked boys. See you monsters on the dance floor!
