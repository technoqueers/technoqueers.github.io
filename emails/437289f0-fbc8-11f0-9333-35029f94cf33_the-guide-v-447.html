Some of this hesitancy has receded as the pro-Palestine movement has gained momentum during the past two years, but unfortunately it’s also led to a rise in performative activism. Seeing people posting a ‘No Music for Genocide’ tile on Instagram or wearing a keffiyeh to appeal to their progressive fanbase while either ignoring or actively agitating against boycotts is a new low."

+ ‘She was a bitch in the best possible way’: the life and mysterious death of drag queen Heklina [Guardian] "By 1996, Heklina was working at The Stud, where she was offered the opportunity to put on an event in the traditionally slow Tuesday night slot. Trannyshack was born, a weekly party where drag shows began at midnight. In the early days, the stage was made from wooden planks stacked on top of beer crates, which were liable to collapse during particularly energetic performances. Back then, drag was still underground. “Numbers would involve blood and vomit and faeces. You never knew what you were going to get.”
