+ 183: Ronen Givony [Herb Sundays] Bryan Kasenic: At that point—in 2007—the city had been cracking down on nightlife, especially in Manhattan, for years. subTonic somehow managed to stay off of their radar for an unbelievably long time. I was pretty oblivious to the fact that throwing a packed techno party down there was illegal in about 17 different ways until the task force came in and wrote us 17 tickets that night. My immediate reaction was one of deep sadness that that particular party was coming to an early end. After years of asking  management to remove the wine barrels to make more room for our expanding party, they finally did it, and this was the very first party with a bigger dancefloor.

+ France proposes jail terms and fines for illegal raves [RA] "Organisers of unlicensed gatherings of more than 250 attendees would face up to six months in prison and fines of €30,000, while attendees could be fined €1,500, rising to €3,000 for repeat offences. The bill could significantly widen the definition of an "organiser," potentially including those who help with logistics, transport sound systems or share event information—extending criminal liability beyond core promoters."
