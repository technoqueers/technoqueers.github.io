Music scenes were once DIY or “indie” until those were co-opted. Will the emerging idea of artistic “interdependence” fare any better? // The independent musician is now an inspirational figure for hegemonic institutions — no longer a countercultural rebel but the gig economy’s ideal worker.

Noticing or prioritizing interdependence isn’t enough to guarantee that these dependencies are organized justly. The music industry has always been interdependent, and those relations have been far from ethical and just.

In these conversations, “interdependence” functions as what philosopher Charles Mills calls an “ideal-as-idealized-model,” which imagines how things ought to work in perfect circumstances but which, in white supremacist societies, reflect and naturalize white people’s experiences of the benefits that accrue to them.

First Floor #25 // Your Hypocrisy Is Showing (a.k.a. The troubling new partnership between Resident Advisor and Spotify.) // I’m not a fan of the streaming economy:

  • The absurdly low payouts to artists (e.g. $.004 per stream from Spotify).

  • The shift in income from artists and labels to tech companies and their employees.

  • The continued (and arguably accelerated) commodification of music, coupled with the ongoing reduction of its perceived value.

  • The rise of the playlist, which further devalues albums and other release formats while frequently obscuring or hiding relevant information such as production and songwriting credits.

  • The primacy of data over expertise, leading to lowest-common-denominator curation in which audience familiarity and broad popularity vastly outweigh the importance of quality and diversity.

  • The ease in which that data can be manipulated and outright falsified, a phenomenon amplified by the lack of checks on those abusing the system.

  • The marketing of music based on mood and other experience-based notions (e.g. “Music for Your Morning Workout”), further elevating the idea of music as a lifestyle accessory / consumer good as opposed to art.

  • The continued teardown of cultural filters (i.e. media, radio, etc.) with an interest in promoting music beyond “whatever is most popular,” creating a playing field in which all artists are competing with one another, from the smallest bedroom songwriter to the biggest international pop star. Given that the latter has infinitely more resources, they’ve become more dominant than ever.
Spotify is not your friend, no matter what Resident Advisor (or anyone else) says.

Remembering Patrick Cowley, Pioneer of Dance Music and Occasional Composer of Porn Soundtracks // Melding psychedelia with disco at a fast tempo, Cowley refined a new music genre: “High-energy,” (stylized as “Hi-NRG”) seemed ideal to enhance the hot and sweaty vibe of San Francisco’s gay clubs. It also influenced the entire music scene, anticipating the house and techno genres that emerged some 15 years later.

A Wendy Carlos biography is on the way // The 264-page biography follows the singular life and career of the Rhode Island native whose work was crucial in bringing synthesizer music to the mainstream.

Carlos, a three-time Grammy winner, underwent gender reassignment surgery in 1979, and also became a transgender icon and pioneer, as one of the first public figures to disclose this procedure.
