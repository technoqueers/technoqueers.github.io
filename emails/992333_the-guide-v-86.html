Creative Footprint is a non-profit initiative from Berlin Clubcommission spokesperson Lutz Leichsenring & Amsterdam Night Mayor Mirik Milan measuring & indexing live music spaces. Lutz & Mirik have armed their nightlife communities & governing bodies with the data needed to support their scenes in the ongoing battle against gentrification. They'll be working with stakeholders in our scene to help us achieve similar successes. Stay tuned for Nightcamp, their hands-on workshop series combining policy, data analysis, & grassroots organizing.

Moogfest reveals first names for 2018, emphasizing female, transgender and non-binary artists // The lineup announcement today coincides with the start of a 50-hour live stream, Always On, showcasing 50 female, transgender, and non-binary electronic artists as they broadcast from 17 different countries. It's hosted by Moogfest and Tom Tom Magazine.

NY Times // The Black Madonna, An Activist D.J., Wants to Turn Dance Music Upside Down // A tireless champion of women and L.G.B.T. people in the club world, the outspoken Ms. Stamper (her Twitter feed is a refreshing delight) used her platform to create initiatives such as Daphne, a series of workshops, gigs and talks at smartbar for women and non-binary participants in March 2015. In the dance and electronic-music world, where women are not just few but also routinely overlooked, Ms. Stamper has become a de facto activist whose call to arms for dance music involved a demand for, among other things, riot grrrls, “women over the age of 40” and “poor people and people who don’t have the right shoes to get into the club.”
