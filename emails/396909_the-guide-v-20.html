(( Mix Of The Week & Artist Profile ))

(( Etapp Kyle ))

Not much is known about Etapp Kyle, the young Berliner by way of Ukraine, apart from his rapid rise from Moscow’s underground techno scene to Berlin’s famed Ostgut Ton label, where he now frequently shares the decks with Marcel Dettman, Steffi, and other Berghain heavy hitters. With the release of Klockworks 10 on Ben Klock’s label in 2013, an EP featuring steely, synth-laden techno tracks tailor-made for the dance floor, Kyle’s international DJ career took off and he began playing regularly in some of Europe’s best clubs. After releasing a second Klockworks EP in 2015, Kyle debuted his first solo EP, a striking collection of widely varied futurist techno tracks dubbed “Continuum”, on the Ostgut sub-label Unterton earlier this year. Intrigue and excitement continue to build around Kyle, fueled by his nonstop touring schedule, and in a few short years he has become one of Berlin’s most buzzed about emerging techno artists.

In this XLR8R podcast, recorded in March of this year, Kyle delivers one hour of pulsating, cerebral, and beautifully textured techno. It’s as if the tracks he strings together form a single, undulating sine wave, expanding and contracting, changing in hue but not in structure. Smooth, rounded kick drum patterns form the bedrock upon which phaser-like beeps, subdued claps, rattling percussion, and astral synth pads weave in and out, equal parts dazzling and restrained. In both this mix and his productions, Kyle favors laser-sharp minimal elements layered in such a way to sustain heart beat-like momentum within a deep, emotive, and delicately crafted sonic landscape. A particularly striking moment of contrast occurs near the halfway point, when low synths emerge from a tight, hypnotic drum loop to signal the arrival of lush, minor-chord strings, atop which the plaintive echo of DJ Rum’s “Mountains - pt 1” vocal sample drifts gently in. From here, a subtle breakbeat and bright, quivering melody arrive to conjure post-peak rave energy, which gradually melts into a blissed-out haze that recalls mid-2000s Aphex Twin. By the mix’s final minutes, you feel gently wrung-out, calm and not unlike a yogi lying supine in Savasana -- no small feat considering the relatively modest tools Kyle has at his disposal.

This Friday, Etapp Kyle will play Unter here in Brooklyn, where he’ll be sharing the bill with fellow rising star and Berghain resident Kobosil. This will be Kyle’s US debut, so don’t miss out on this special opportunity. See you there!
