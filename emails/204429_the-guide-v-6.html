Techno Queers NY

This edition of the guide is dedicated to all of you degenerate rave sluts who are unable to make it to Movement this year. What's amazing is that this exodus to Detroit hardly results in tumbleweeds on the dancefloor. In fact, New York is itself a music festival that never stops, masquerading as a city.

If you will be Movement this weekend: see you at Club Toilet

Sign up for our weekly emails: technoqueers.com
(( MIX OF THE WEEK))
(( DJ Shiva & Clark Price ))
For over two decades, Indianapolis-based DJ Shiva has been bringing techno to dancefloors across the U.S. For the last two years, she has been hosting techno talent from around the world via her internet show SUBterror Radio. This time she hosts Clark Price, a staple of Pittsburgh's Honcho parties, who you can catch in Detroit this weekend at Club Toilet!
Thursday
TECHNOFEMINISM // Special guests Buck Smith and Elisabeth Dalton are joining mainstays Beta Librae and Umfang for another fog-fueled mind-melt! If you're part of the Movement crowd, let this be your intimate send-off before losing yourself in a sea of 100K+ ravers.
LIES Records presents Ron Morelli, Adam X, Jahiliyya Fields (Live) plus Further Reductions DJ set at Good Room // This NY-centric L.I.E.S. lineup is absurdly stacked for a Thursday night. Do not miss Adam X, a pioneer of NY's techno scene in the early 90's who is now based in Berlin.
Friday
Len Faki | Lee K (5:27:16) Flash Factory // We wanted to launch a queer takeover of this event to show Len Faki some love, but with the looming Movement exodus, we decided against it. Regardless, we encourage you to check it out and report back! We’re still trying pin down the enigma that is Flash Factory: bottle service in Chelsea, yet also “for the people”? If anything is certain, it’s that Len Faki will deliver.
Saturday
The Long Count Presents… Kerri LeBon (Jealous God / Nation / Discipline) // Don’t miss Kerri LeBon, resident DJ of the DISCIPLINE party in the Bay Area. Strap on your leather gear -- but not too much, because now it’s hot outside -- and get ready to be plunged into the void. Rounding out her pounding techno sounds will be the always mind-blowing Scallywag & Blacklauren (The Long Count Cycle).
Oscillate /III/ feat. Patricia // Malory // Max McFerren // Plebeian // This left-of-center lineup comprised of local talent, new and old, is gonna make you sweat! Consider this Brooklyn’s home-brewed antidote to your Movement-related FOMO. Expect a variety of soundscapes and an intimate crowd.
Still thirsty?
FRIDAY // The Party by Ostbahnhof NYC // Have you ever wished that techno music had more sequins? More aerial performance? More cage dancers? Drag queens? That the bathrooms at your techno show worked, and were in fact works of art? Consider this event a pastiche in which you will find your niche.
SATURDAY // RECON XXV // Thirsty for music that’s dark and danceable, but isn’t techno? Check out this extremely well-curated, high production value event. Expect industrial, post-grime, experimental, dub, and more!
