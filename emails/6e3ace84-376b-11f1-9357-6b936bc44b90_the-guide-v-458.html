+ Enshittification +

+ San Francisco's techno boom is a warning to the world [No Tags] "AEG is owned by billionaire Philip Anschutz, a conservative Christian megadonor to anti-queer causes. An ironic disparity, given that the history of dance music is inseparable from queer community. That all seems beside the point, though, when a crowd of 20,000 people enjoying $18 vodka-sodas loses themselves to a DJ in a horse mask playing hardstyle remixes of pop songs. And yet the underground never dies, even in San Francisco."

+ How Big Tech Co-opted DIY – and how to Fight Back [Silicon Valley made everyone think we could operate alone] [tQ] "Big Tech has managed to ensure that for the most part, everyone’s first thought when contemplating how to reach people is to utilise the platforms Silicon Valley owns. Almost everyone, it seems, has fallen under the spell. More and more artists are shunning social media, recognising that it is a race to the bottom."

+ Gentrifying the Underground: The Hidden Class Divide in Electronic Dance Music [Midnight Rebels] "We are stuck in the era of TikTok Techno. DJs prioritize social media metrics over musical depth. They wear all black and act edgy while playing music they did not even write. It is a cynical pantomime of rebellion."

+ ‘This craving to go viral is tiresome’: the artists sick of the pressure to promote on social media [Guardian] "82% of all internet traffic is now made up of videos." "We’re at a real crossroads. The worst people on earth control the means of communication."

+ The experience economy arms race and the end of the recording artist [No Tags] "You can generate a song in less time than it takes to listen to one, or generate a video in less time than it takes to watch it. So ultimately, that’s going to be dominant mode of production. I don’t really see any other way around it."
