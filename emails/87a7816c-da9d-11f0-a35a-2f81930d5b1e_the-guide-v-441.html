+ @queer_happened_here:
+ The Outside Inn [Operating from at least the late 1960s through the 1970s, The Outside Inn was an early gay-identified nightclub & performance venue in Jackson Heights, Queens, at 82-20 Baxter Avenue. […] It featured numerous Bose speakers suspended from the ceiling, part of Richard Long’s very 1st sound system installation. Long’s systems would later define many of NYC’s most influential dancefloors, including at Paradise Garage, The Loft, & Studio 54. Long died of AIDS in 1986.]
+ Ronnie’s Roost [A gay bar/lounge open circa 1974-1975 inside the Hotel Beacon at 2130 Broadway on Manhattan’s Upper West Side.]
+ g Lounge [First opened in 1996, g Lounge–often called GBar–was a defining venue of Chelsea’s rise as NYC’s primary gay nightlife district, known for its circular “g”-shaped bar, daylight-filled interior, & polished lounge atmosphere.]
+ Ladylike [A queer-centered dance party active primarily from 2016 to 2019, founded & hosted by hairstylist and cultural organizer Masami Hosono, best known for their gender-neutral East Village salon Vacancy Project.]
+ Ramrod Male Theatre [not to be confused with Ramrod bar or Ramrod Showplace] [Located at 210 W. 49th St, it was one of several gay male porn cinemas that operated in NYC during the mid-1970s & early ‘80s. Before adopting the Ramrod name, the venue briefly operated as the 49th Street Burlesk in 1974–75, showing adult burlesque & erotic films. By the late 1970s, it became a fixture in Times Square’s once-dense network of adult cinemas.]
+ R.I.P. Rosa von Praunheim [German filmmaker, author, producer, professor & activist whose relationship with NYC spanned decades, with multiple films of his made in & about the city itself. […] Von Praunheim ultimately made over 150 movies, many of which centered on queer-related themes & are now considered landmark films, including A Virus Knows No Morals (1986), I Am My Own Woman (1992) Transexual Menace (1996), The Einstein of Sex (1999), & Rent Boys (2011)]
