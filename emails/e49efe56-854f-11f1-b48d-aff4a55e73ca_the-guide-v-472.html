+ Joy as Resistance +

+ The INFERNO Guide To Raving [A free publication exploring care, community and the future of queer nightlife] "We created this guide because we believe everyone contributes to the atmosphere of a night. Whether you're stepping into queer nightlife for the very first time, have been dancing for decades or are building spaces of your own, this guide offers an invitation to think differently about what a rave can be."

+ When joy was resistance on the dance floor [What we can learn from queer nightlife during the AIDS crisis] [Salon] "Survival is not only the refusal to die. It is the insistence on remaining human while threatened."

"The word “joy” can be misleading if we make it too soft. Joy in queer nightlife was and is sweaty, sexual, improvised, defiant, sometimes reckless, sometimes holy. It did not erase grief. It gave grief somewhere to go."

"None of this means dancing is enough. A party will not stop authoritarianism. A drag show will not repeal anti-LGBTQ legislation. A nightclub cannot replace courts, organizing, voting, or protest. Joy without vigilance can become anesthesia. Joy without community can become branding. Joy without politics can become escape for those privileged enough to leave others behind."

+ Kevin Aviance survived an antigay hate crime. Twenty years later, he warns LGBTQ+ people not to disappear [The queer dance music icon reflects on the 2006 beating that nearly ended his career and why queer and trans people must gather, resist, and live openly] [Advocate] "Look out for each other, especially Black trans women, drag artists, and others whose visibility makes them targets."

I’m lucky enough to have a platform that reaches many people, so I felt like this was the perfect time to assemble a team with people from all those groups. Make our voices loud when politically they’re being silenced."

+ Drugs +

+ Can I Go Three Weeks Off Adderall? [Geoffrey Mak] "When you are experiencing a craving, the body is responding to the anticipation of satisfaction, rather than the actual experience of what you’re craving. This is why we still crave certain substances, even long after the substance has long stopped providing meaningful satisfaction."

"I tell people my favorite neighborhood in Brooklyn is Ridgewood, but it’s secretly Williamsburg. This is entirely based off of Bathhouse Williamsburg, which is the peak of Kamala liberalism. HBO creatives talking Baudrillard at the sauna. I’m told that buried beneath the floorboards is a giant crypto mining computer. You go there to secrete toxins to the literal warmth of money."

+ The Quiet, Curious Return of Quaaludes [Vice] "The drug was unexpectedly adopted by the counterculture thanks to its heavenly effects—even by hippies who had previously drawn a moral line between their parents’ soul-deadening downers and their own consciousness-expanding stashes of weed and LSD. By the early 70s, with prescriptions estimated in the millions, Quaaludes had escaped the doctor’s office and taken over the dancefloor, referred to as “disco biscuits” and prized for their loosening, inhibitionless buzz—one ripe for potentially catastrophic impairment."
